A few things
before we start.

This isn't a checklist. It's an invitation to show up honestly.

Bring something real with you
  • A project you keep almost starting — but never quite do
  • Something you're waiting to feel "ready" for, even though ready never quite arrives
  • A habit you've tried to build three times and abandoned each time
  • A version of yourself you perform at work that doesn't feel like the whole picture
  • A goal you achieved that didn't feel the way you expected it to
  • A relationship dynamic you've noticed but haven't named out loud yet

You don't need to have it figured out. You don't need to know which one applies. Just bring whatever is most alive for you right now.

Living MESSY isn't a technique. It's what happens when the five practices stop being something you do in a course and start being how you pay attention to your life.

The relationship dynamic example — something real, cross-domain, not yet resolved — is a Living MESSY moment. It doesn't fit neatly into Monitor or Surrender or Yield. It belongs to all of them at once. That's the point. Real life doesn't arrive one dimension at a time.

The ∫ symbol is an integral sign — from mathematics. The integral accumulates. It's the sum of everything underneath the curve, across time. That's exactly what Living MESSY is: the accumulated wisdom of every cycle of the framework you've moved through, applied to the full texture of your actual life.

The course ends. The practice doesn't.

Used only on the completion screen. Never stored anywhere else.

Quick self-check — no right answers

Three questions to help you notice where you are starting from. Nothing is submitted or scored. There is no right answer.

1. When a personal development system stops working for you, what do you usually do?
2. How comfortable are you right now with things in your life being unresolved?
3. When was the last time you shared something unfinished or imperfect with someone you trust?

Notice what your answers reveal — not what they say about you, but what they say about where you are right now. That is the starting point.
